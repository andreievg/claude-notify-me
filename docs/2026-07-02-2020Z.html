Solana Launches On-Chain Governance System — 100,000 SOL ($7.7M) Required to Submit Proposals

2 Jul 2026

Solana activated Solana Governance Proposals (SGPs) on July 2, introducing a formal on-chain governance system for the first time. Validators must have at least 100,000 SOL (~$7.7M at current prices) staked to open a proposal; proposals reach a community ballot once they attract 15% active-stake support, and passage requires a two-thirds supermajority with no minimum turnout. The system introduces “staker sovereignty,” allowing individual delegators to override their validator’s vote or cast ballots independently if their validator abstains, keeping voting power with token holders rather than concentrated among validators. SGPs handle high-level “should we do this” decisions and are separate from the existing Solana Improvement Document (SIMD) technical track. SOL was up approximately 16% on the week leading into the launch.
