On-Chain Governance (SGPs) Live on Mainnet; MoneyGram Joins Solana as Validator

2–3 Jul 2026

Solana Governance Proposals (SGPs) activated on July 2, giving the network its first formal on-chain governance mechanism. Validators must have at least 100,000 SOL (~$7.7M at current prices) staked to open a proposal; a proposal advances to a community ballot after attracting 15% active-stake support and passes with a two-thirds supermajority. A key design feature is “staker sovereignty”: individual delegators can override their validator’s vote, keeping governance power distributed rather than concentrated among large validators. SGPs govern high-level strategic decisions and operate separately from the existing SIMD technical track. SOL gained approximately 10% on the day SGPs launched. In parallel, MoneyGram announced it has joined Solana as a validator, a development that bolsters stablecoin payment infrastructure and institutional credibility for the network.
