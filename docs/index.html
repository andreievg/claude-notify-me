Japan Reclassifies Crypto as Financial Asset; Opens Path for Bitcoin ETFs and 20% Flat Tax

15 Jul 2026

Japan’s upper house approved legislation on 15 Jul 2026 moving crypto regulation under the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act (FIEA) — the same framework that governs stocks and bonds. The law creates the legal foundation for spot Bitcoin ETFs (no products approved yet; the FSA will now develop a regulatory framework) and cuts the top crypto tax rate from as high as 55% to a flat 20%, both effective from 2028. Bitcoin ETFs could list on the Tokyo Stock Exchange by late 2027 or 2028. The move reshapes Japan’s crypto landscape from a payment-focused regime to an investment one and is a meaningful long-term institutional demand driver for Bitcoin and large-cap crypto assets.
