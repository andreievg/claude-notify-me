Japan Reclassifies Crypto as Financial Asset; Opens Path for Bitcoin ETFs and 20% Flat Tax

15 Jul 2026

Japan’s upper house approved legislation on 15 Jul 2026 moving crypto regulation under the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act (FIEA) — the same framework that governs stocks and bonds. The law creates the legal framework for spot Bitcoin ETFs (no products approved yet; a regulatory framework will now be developed by the FSA), and cuts the top crypto tax rate from as high as 55% to a flat 20%, both effective from 2028. Bitcoin ETFs could list on the Tokyo Stock Exchange by late 2027 or 2028. The move reshapes Japan’s crypto landscape from a payment-focused regime to an investment one, and is a meaningful long-term institutional demand driver for Bitcoin and large-cap crypto assets. SBI Holdings announced on 13 Jul that it is redirecting its blockchain initiative from R3’s Corda to the Solana network, renaming SBI R3 Japan to SBI Solana Global.
