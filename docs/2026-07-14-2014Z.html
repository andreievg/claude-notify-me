U.S. Government Moves $288M in Seized BTC and ETH to Coinbase Prime — Potential Liquidation Overhang

14 Jul 2026

The U.S. government transferred approximately $288 million in seized digital assets to Coinbase Prime on 14 Jul: ~3,800 BTC ($235M) drawn from the Ryan Farace “Xanaxman” dark-web case and the defunct BTC-e exchange, routed through intermediary wallets; and ~30,000 ETH ($53M) from a money-laundering case involving Oracle employee Brian Krewson, sent directly. The transfers reignite the liquidation-overhang debate, as Coinbase Prime is the U.S. Marshals’ designated custody-and-trade platform. However, the moves appear to conflict with President Trump’s March 2025 executive order directing seized BTC into the Strategic Bitcoin Reserve and barring its sale; it remains unclear whether the transfers are for sale preparation or simple custody consolidation. The $288M represents a small fraction of the government’s estimated $20.65B in total crypto holdings. Not investment advice.
