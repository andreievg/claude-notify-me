NZ Severe Weather Watch

Over 5–6 June 2026, MetService issued orange heavy rain warnings across the North Island — covering Taranaki, Eastern Bay of Plenty, Tararua Range, and Bay of Plenty east of Whakatāne — as a humid northerly trough delivered heavy, thundery rainfall. Severe thunderstorm watches were also issued for Northland, Auckland, Great Barrier Island, Waikato, and Coromandel, warning of damaging wind gusts up to 100 km/h and a risk of localised tornadoes. Wellington faced 26 consecutive hours of rain from midnight 5–6 June, and additional heavy rain watches extended to the western Bay of Plenty, Central North Island, and Horowhenua. Conditions began easing across most of the North Island by 7 June.
