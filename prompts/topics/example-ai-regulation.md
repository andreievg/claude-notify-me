## What to track
New laws, regulatory actions, enforcement decisions, and major official policy statements
that affect how AI is developed or deployed. Include binding rules, proposed legislation
that has materially advanced, formal regulatory inquiries, and significant agency guidance.
Exclude product launches, funding rounds, and general industry opinion.

## Summarization criteria
- Lead with the concrete action: who did what, when.
- Prefer the primary/official source (the regulation, ruling, or agency statement).
- One or two factual sentences per item; no editorializing.

## Severity rubric
- **critical** — a binding law/regulation takes effect, or enforcement with immediate broad
  impact (bans, large fines, mandatory compliance dates).
- **high** — legislation formally advancing, a formal regulatory inquiry opened, or major
  agency guidance issued.
- **medium** — official statements of intent, public consultations, or draft proposals.
- **low** — commentary, analysis, speculation, or minor procedural updates.
